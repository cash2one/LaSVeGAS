612888:::::The Lonely Londoners:::::From the brilliant, sharp, witty pen of Sam Selvon, his classic award-winning novel of immigrant life in London in the 1950s. 'His Lonely Londoners has acquired a classic status since it appeared in 1956 as the definitive novel about London's West Indians.' Financial Times 'The unforgettable picaresque . . . a vernacular comedy of pathos.' The Guardian In the hopeful aftermath of war they flocked to the Mother Country West Indians in search of a prosperous future in the 'glitter-city'. Instead, they have to face the harsh realities of living hand to mouth, of racism, of bone-chilling weather and bleak prospects. Yet friendships flourish among these Lonely Londoners and, in time, they learn to survive.
