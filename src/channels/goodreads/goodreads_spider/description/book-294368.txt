294368:::::The Lives of a Cell: Notes of a Biology Watcher:::::Anticipates the kind of writing that will appear more frequently as scientists take on poetic language in order to communicate human truths too mysterious for old-fashioned commonsense. Elegant, suggestive &amp; clarifying, Thomas' humane vision explores the world &amp; examines the complex interdependence of all things. Extending beyond the usual limitations of biological science &amp; into a vast world of hidden relationships, this book explores in personal, poetic essays to topics such as computers, germs, language, music, death, insects &amp; medicine. He writes, "Once you have become permanently startled, as I am, by the realization that we are a social species, you tend to keep an eye out for the pieces of evidence that this is, by &amp; large, good for us."
